Description

Information technology is the study of how to integrate hardware and software to create computing solutions that meet the demands of different types of users and organizations. A wide range of knowledge areas, from organizational information systems to systems infrastructure, are covered in the Information Technology (IT) program in order to prepare graduates to choose, apply, configure, integrate, deploy, and administer computing technology in order to meet user requirements in a variety of contexts. With their strong technical, managerial, and soft skills, BSIT graduates are well-suited for positions as network engineers, system administrators, information security specialists, technology consultants, and enterprise system specialists.

Course Offer

The BSIT program at QCU-San Francisco is a four-year undergraduate degree program that equips students with the technical knowledge and skills needed to design, develop, implement, and manage information systems. It emphasizes practical application and industry-relevant skills to prepare graduates for successful careers in the IT field.

Facilities:
The QCU-San Francisco campus has modern IT facilities, including computer labs equipped with the latest hardware and software, a network operations center, and a research laboratory. These facilities provide students with a hands-on learning environment where they can practice their skills and gain practical experience.

Career and Skills

What Jobs Can You Pursue With an Information Technology Degree?
Graduates of the BSIT program at QCU-San Francisco are well-prepared for a variety of careers in the IT field. Some popular career paths include: Software developer, Web developer, Network administrator, Database administrator, Systems analyst, IT project manager, Information security analyst, Data analyst Game developer. The demand for IT professionals is growing rapidly, and graduates of the BSIT program at QCU-San Francisco are in high demand by employers.
